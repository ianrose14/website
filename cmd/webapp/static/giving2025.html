Giving 2025

As 2026 dawns, I thought I'd try something new to close out 2025. I've tried to be a bit more intentional about charitable giving this year, helped in part by more extensive use of DAFs (donor-advised funds) -- I've found that (i) DAFs made it easy to manage multiple monthly donations all from one place, and (ii) the psychology of "this money is already dedicated to charity" made it easier to give freely.

I'm frequently curious about how other people think about charitable giving. Do you choose to give more now, or keep funds invested with a plan to give more later? How do you balance local vs global causes? What do you think about "Effective Altruism"? (It's gotten some bad press recently - e.g. thanks to conman Sam Bankman-Fried - but I think it's still an interesting idea worth debating.)

In Peter Singer's book, The Life You Can Save, he advocates for a culture of giving, including via social media. Publicly discussing charitable giving is difficult for most of us (it smacks of attention-seeking, and a bit self-congratulatory), but if you overcome this reticence, you are (theoretically) helping to normalize discussion around giving, and hopefully encouraging others to give more as well. As such, here's my attempt...

What follows is a list of most of the organizations that I donated to in 2025. I decided not to include actual dollar amounts, as that was a step too far for me. And in particular, I think most would agree that your "responsibility" grows with your means, so that's going to be different for everyone. Peter Singer provides his own take on this via an income-based rubric for giving amounts. I did not intentionally follow this advice at all during the year, but now that I've added things up, I'm glad to see that I comfortably met his criterion.

Let me know what you think. Did I miss some important charities? Are there substitutions I should consider? Do you have a fundamentally different approach or philosophy of giving? I'd love to hear your thoughts. If you are reading this page, you probably already have my e-mail or phone number...

Local Organizations

  • Our House - Aiding women and children facing homelessness around Atlanta.
  • Atlanta Community Food Bank - Working to end hunger in the Greater Atlanta area.
  • El Refugio - A hospitality house in Lumpkin, GA that provides a place to stay for those visiting immigrants at the Steward Detention center.

National Organizations

  • Southern Environmental Law Center (SELC) - Fighting against Trump's policies. Previously I was a Sierra Club supporter, but the SELC feels like they have a more focused approach.
  • Planned Parenthood Federation - Fighting more Trump policies, and supporting reproductive rights wherever still possible.

International Organizations

  • Akshaya Patra Foundation USA - Providing school meals for millions of Indian school children. I've supported this organization for 15 years, ever since attending a wedding in India where the bride and groom asked for donations in lieu of gifts.
  • The Life You Can Save - Formed around the model of Peter Singer's book, distributes funds to a collection of vetted, high-impact charities.
  • Fistula Foundation - Surgical care for women suffering from obstetric fistula, predominately in Africa.
  • water.org - Clean water and sanitation to communities around the world.

Ukrainian Organizations

  • US Ukrainian Activists - Humanitarian aid, particular supporting the Hospitallers, front-line paramedics.
  • Bird of Light Foundation - Community, civilian and infrastructure support in Ukraine.
  • Come Back Alive - Providing military equipment to Ukrainian defense forces.

Schools

  • Dartmouth College - Dartmouth has plenty of cash, but with kids approaching nearing college, gotta support the alma mater, just in case!
  • St. Andrew's School - A gift in memory of Allison; not only was she both a student and a faculty member at St. Andrew's, but it was where we were married and lived for our first year (as dorm parents).
